Mum's the Word
She's the reel mom to stars. Lakshmy Ramakrishnan, 42, has played `mother' to Tamil actors such as Trisha and Arya. She even tonsured her head for her role as a vengeful mother in the Tamil thriller Yuddham Sei, which released in 2011. Now, in an all-new avatar, Ramakrishnan is gearing up for the July release of her directorial debut Arohanam. However, she isn't out of her comfort zone as yet, for her new film is about, yes you guessed right--motherhood!

ARTISTIC TURN
She was briefly seen in Ram Gopal Varma's horror film Agyaat, in 2009. Meet actor Rasika Dugal, 28, who plays Chhaya, an artist obsessed with an unfinished sculpture of Goddess Lakshmi, and haunted by the demons of her past, in Karan Gour's Kshay. Kshay won the Best Feature Film Award at the Indian Film Festival of Los Angeles held from April 10-15. Her next project is the Indo-European film Qissa, alongside Irrfan. Dugal also did a stint in theatre, with minor roles in The Vagina Monologues and Bombay Talkies. "Acting only happened on a whim," she says. DUGAL
